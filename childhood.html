Transitional Childhood

Introduction: Our project focuses on the untold stories of transnational childhood. These stories revolve around experiences of migration and resettlement of migrant children. Unlike adult migrants, children migrants donâ€™t always have the choice or agency to move to another country. Some of the push factors and extraneous circumstances may consist of domestic violence, lost of family members, lack of resources, and unstable political climates. Despite their ability to adapt to the new culture and language at a young age, many still encounter unique challenges such as language barriers with family members, limited access to health care, poor conditions, and discrimination in the educational system. Yet migrant children are able to preserve and have a great work ethic to reach their goals.
