Diasporas of Migration

For much of class we considered the act, process, and status of migration and being migratory. Our project considers the roots that take hold after migrating - the establishment of diaspora. The project has two components. The first, a map, is an interactive piece for classmates and visitors to map their own trails. Where have you come from, and where do you call home now? The second component is a zine. The articles cover our reflections on family, ancestors, food, discrimination - aka, daily life in the diaspora. On the cover, we extend the definition of diaspora: "we who belong in multiple spaces, we who belong nowhere." We hope this zine might be a reminder that if we belong nowhere, we do not belong nowhere alone
