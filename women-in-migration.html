Women in Migration

Migrant women interviewed for this project

This project includes the voices of women in migration from all over the globe. Women have commonly been depicted as companions to men in the narrative of migration. Men are seen as the active migrants while women are viewed as their sisters, wives, daughters, etc. We wanted to combat this notion of women as passive in the narrative of migration. We conducted interviews with migrants in our families (including our grandmothers and mothers) to examine what roles these women played in these specific narratives of migration. Women are the cultural agents who have to negotiate their own culture within the context of the new culture they are living in. Our project hopes to highlight this by sharing their voices as they describe what it was like to bridge their country of origin with their new home.
